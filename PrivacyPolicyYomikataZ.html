Privacy Policy — Yomikata Z

Last updated: 22 June 2026

Yomikata Z (“the app”) is a free, open-source Japanese-learning app developed by Valentin Lanfranchi (“I”, “me”). This policy explains what data the app collects, why, the legal basis for processing it, and the rights you have under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and similar laws.

Data controller: Valentin Lanfranchi — contact: jehutyno@gmail.com

1. Summary

  • The app works fully offline. Your learning progress and statistics are stored only on your device and are never sent to me.
  • Optional, anonymous usage analytics and crash reporting are provided by Google Firebase. They are turned off by default and are only activated if you give your explicit consent.
  • I do not collect your name, email, account, contacts, location, or any other data that directly identifies you, and I never sell data.

2. Data stored only on your device

Your quiz progress, word mastery levels, selections (custom word lists) and personal statistics are stored locally on your device. This data is not transmitted to me or to any third party. It is removed when you uninstall the app (you can back it up or reset it yourself from the app’s settings).

3. Optional analytics and crash reporting (consent-based)

To understand how the app is used and to fix bugs, the app can use two Google Firebase services. Both are disabled by default. The first time you open the app you are asked whether you agree to enable them, and you can change your choice at any time in Settings → Usage statistics. If you decline, no analytics or crash data is collected or sent.

3.1 Firebase Analytics

When enabled, it collects anonymous, aggregated usage events, such as:

  • app launches and session frequency;
  • which quiz types and which study levels are used;
  • whether the “selections” feature is used (creation/deletion, quizzes launched);
  • quiz completion (session length, strategy, number of errors);
  • standard technical context automatically added by Firebase: app version, device model, operating-system version, language, and a coarse region derived from the IP address (the IP address itself is not stored by me).

These events contain no personal content — no words you studied, no names you gave to your selections, and no user identifier that I can link back to you.

3.2 Firebase Crashlytics

When enabled, if the app crashes it sends a crash report (stack trace, app state, device model and OS version) so I can diagnose and fix the problem. No personal content is included.

4. Other third-party connection

The home screen may load a short news message and support configuration from Firebase Realtime Database. This is a one-way read of public content; no personal data about you is sent or collected in the process. Voice packs, when you choose to download them, are fetched from GitHub Releases.

5. Legal basis

The legal basis for collecting analytics and crash-reporting data is your consent (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)), which is requested as an explicit opt-in and can be withdrawn at any time. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before the withdrawal.

6. Data processor and international transfers

Analytics and crash data are processed by Google (Firebase) acting as a data processor on my behalf. Google may process this data on servers located outside your country, including in the United States, under appropriate safeguards. See Google’s privacy policy at policies.google.com/privacy and Firebase’s data handling at firebase.google.com/support/privacy.

7. Data retention

Firebase Analytics data is retained according to the configured retention period in the Firebase/Google Analytics console (a limited number of months) and is then automatically deleted. Crash reports are retained for a limited time for debugging purposes. On-device data is kept until you reset it or uninstall the app.

8. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, or object to the processing of your personal data, and the right to data portability. Because the analytics data is anonymous and not linked to an identifier I hold, I may be unable to single out your individual records; in that case the most effective way to stop all collection is to disable usage statistics in Settings or to uninstall the app. For any request or question, contact me at jehutyno@gmail.com. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority.

9. Children’s privacy

The app is not directed to children under 13 and I do not knowingly collect personal data from them. If you believe a child has provided personal data, contact me and I will take appropriate action.

10. Changes to this policy

I may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes are published on this page with an updated “Last updated” date and take effect when posted.

11. Contact

For any question about this policy or your data, contact Valentin Lanfranchi at jehutyno@gmail.com.
